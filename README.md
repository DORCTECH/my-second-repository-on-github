# 📊 Data Professional Survey Breakdown Dashboard
## Overview

The Data Professional Survey Breakdown Dashboard is an interactive Power BI project that analyzes survey responses from data professionals across different countries, job roles, programming languages, and career experiences.

The objective of this dashboard is to transform raw survey data into meaningful insights that help understand salary trends, workforce demographics, career satisfaction, and the skills commonly used within the data industry.

## Project Objectives

**This dashboard was created to:**

-Analyze salary distribution across different data-related job titles.
-Compare survey responses by country.
-Explore the most popular programming languages among data professionals.
-Measure work-life balance satisfaction.
-Measure salary satisfaction.
-Understand how difficult professionals found it to enter the data industry.
-Present key demographic statistics in an easy-to-understand dashboard.

## Key Insights
**🌍 Country Distribution**

The survey received responses from professionals across multiple countries.

The United States contributed the largest number of respondents, followed by India, while several other countries made up the remaining survey participants.

**💼 Average Salary by Job Title**

The dashboard compares average salaries among different data professions.

**Key observations include:**

-Data Scientists reported the highest average salaries.

-Data Engineers ranked second in salary.

-Data Architects also earned competitive salaries.

-Data Analysts earned comparatively lower salaries than Data Scientists and Engineers.

-Students and individuals seeking employment reported the lowest income levels.

**💻 Favorite Programming Language**

The survey highlights programming language preferences among different job roles.

Major findings include:

Python is by far the most popular programming language.
R is commonly used but significantly less popular than Python.
Languages such as Java, JavaScript, and C/C++ represent only a small percentage of responses.

**🚀 Difficulty Entering Data**

Survey participants rated how difficult it was to break into the data industry.

Most respondents indicated that entering the field was:

-Neither easy nor difficult

-Difficult

-Easy


Only a small percentage described the transition as very difficult.

**😊 Work-Life Balance**

The dashboard reports an average Work-Life Balance Rating of:
**5.74 / 10**

This suggests that respondents experience a moderate level of satisfaction with balancing work and personal life.

**💰 Salary Satisfaction**

Participants rated their satisfaction with their salaries.

Average rating:
**4.27 / 10**

This indicates that many professionals feel their compensation could be improved despite working in the data industry.

**👤 Demographics**

Average respondent age:
**29.87 years**

This suggests that many survey participants are early to mid-career professionals.
